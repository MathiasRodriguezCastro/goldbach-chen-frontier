\noindent This supplement records, in compact form, the cross-disciplinary reframings of the
main paper, \emph{The Goldbach--Chen Frontier: A Singular-Series Cancellation behind a Finite-Range Half-Power}, together
with the empirical probes moved out of the body. Each reframing recasts the \emph{same}
singular-series governance in a different external language; we keep them for the individual
vocabularies and the one honest (structural) negative, not as independent results. ``The main text'' refers
to that paper, and every number is reproducible from the \texttt{run\_*.py} drivers in the
repository.
\medskip

\section{An honest structural negative}
\label{sec:reframings-brief}

The frontier admits many external recastings---thermodynamic (an arithmetic ground state),
network (adversarial robustness), control (value of information), information-theoretic, and
geometric. Each is the same fact---$\sing(N)$ sets the local densities---seen in a different
mirror, with no mathematical content of its own, so we do not develop them (they are reproducible
from the \texttt{run\_*.py} drivers). One recasting, however, returns a genuine \emph{structural}
negative worth recording, because it bounds what the additive structure does \emph{not} buy.
\emph{Persistent topology:} for the binary problem the additive-complexity sublevel filtration is a
disjoint matching, so its $0$-dimensional persistence is just the energy spectrum and all higher
homology vanishes---genuine higher-order topological structure appears only at the ternary level,
which is anti-clustered.

\section{Further empirical probes (moved from the main text)}
\label{sec:moved-probes}

For length and focus, the following experiments were moved out of the main text. Each is
reproducible from its \texttt{run\_*.py} driver in the repository and, like every reframing
here, returns to the same singular series $\sing(N)$.

\subsection{The Chen-rescue threshold}
\label{sec:moved-opt}

Restricting representations to a balanced band $p\in[N/2-k,\,N/2]$ and measuring the fraction
of $N$ that \emph{require} a semiprime (no prime pair in the band) gives a unimodal curve in
$k$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:s-rescue}). For $k\gtrsim0.005\,N$ the prime channel essentially never
fails; the rescue fraction peaks near $38$--$40\%$ at $k\sim20$--$40$; for $k=1,2$ it falls
again because often no representation exists at all. The peak shifts right with $N$ (roughly as
$\log^2N$), where the expected number of balanced prime pairs
$\sim2C_2\,\sing(N)\,k/\log^2(N/2)$ drops to $O(1)$. This is the regime captured by the
residual exponent $a_{\rm Res}(N)$ of the main text: Chen's flexibility becomes \emph{necessary}
precisely when near-perfect balance is imposed, so the rescue threshold and $a_{\rm Res}$ track
the same singular series.

\caption{Fraction of $N$ that require a semiprime rescue versus band half-width $k$, for three
scales of $N$. The curve is unimodal with peak scaling like $\log^2N$.}

\end{figure}

\subsection{Weak continuity of the location measures}
\label{sec:moved-cont}

The pointwise measure $\mu_N$ of $q/N$ is noisy, but averaging over a window $N\in[X,X+H]$
produces a stable density (Fig.~\ref{fig:s-continuity}). At $X=10^{6}$: \emph{(i)} splitting a
window into two same-scale subsamples, the total-variation distance between their densities
falls monotonically from $2.1\cdot10^{-3}$ to $3\cdot10^{-4}$ as $H$ grows from $125$ to
$4000$ (a concrete form of weak convergence); \emph{(ii)} the prime-$q$ density is within
total variation $0.028$ of uniform on $(0,1)$, with a mild endpoint upturn (the
$1/(\log(xN)\log((1-x)N))$ effect); \emph{(iii)} prime and semiprime locations agree to total
variation $0.019$, the semiprime location slightly skewed toward larger $q$ (mean $q/N=0.513$
versus $0.500$ for primes). This probe earns its place because it is explained by the deferred
analytic model of \S\ref{sec:weak-cont} below: under the weighted Hardy--Littlewood asymptotics,
after normalization both the inherited singular series \emph{and} the Mertens channel sum cancel,
leaving the uniform law for $q/N$ in either channel---the same cancellation that drives the whole
paper, now visible in the location law.

\subsection{Topology of valleys}
\label{sec:moved-tda}

Normalising each count by its own singular factor ($R_1/\sing$,
$R_{\le2}/(\sing+\sing^{1/2}W)$) and computing the $0$-dimensional sublevel persistence of the
detrended sequences, the valleys of the Goldbach and Chen comets largely \emph{coincide}: the
anomalies correlate at $+0.64$, and at the $200$ most fragile $N$ the Chen anomaly ($0.29$) is
as deep as the Goldbach one ($0.30$). Chen does \emph{not} topologically fill the Goldbach
valleys (only the deepest $30\%$ are softened, Fig.~\ref{fig:s-tda}); the rescue is one of
magnitude, not of valley structure, because both channels share the constraint that $p$ be
prime. The correlation $+0.64$ is a within-window descriptive statistic over a strongly
arithmetically dependent sequence (consecutive even $N$), not an i.i.d.\ estimate.

normalised anomalies coincide ($+0.64$).}

\subsection{Statistical dynamics of the frontier}
\label{sec:moved-dyn}

The prime share is almost a deterministic function of the singular series: $\theta(N)$ is
$94\%$ explained by $\sing(N)$ alone (adding $N\bmod30$ contributes nothing further), and the
increment $\Delta\theta_N$ is $98\%$ explained by the jump $\sing(N)\to\sing(N+2)$. The
residual is small ($25\%$ of the spread of $\theta$) and is \emph{not} white noise (lag-$1$
autocorrelation $-0.21$ after detrending): the frontier carries little irreducible randomness,
being governed by the local arithmetic of $N$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:s-dynamics}). These percentages
are descriptive $R^2$ values over an arithmetically dependent window, not i.i.d.\ fits.

\section{Deferred analytic models (from the main appendix)}
\label{sec:deferred-models}

These two model/conditional results were moved here from the main appendix to keep it to
three parts; they explain, respectively, the weak-continuity probe and the carry signature
of the main text.

\subsection{Weak uniformity of the location measures}
\label{sec:weak-cont}

The continuity experiments (supplement) suggest that the representation clouds become
continuous after normalization: the empirical laws of $q/N$ for both prime and semiprime $q$
approach the uniform law on $(0,1)$, up to small endpoint effects. This is what the weighted
Hardy--Littlewood heuristic predicts. For a bounded $C^1$ test function
$\varphi:[0,1]\to\mathbb R$ write, when $R_t(N)\ne0$,
\[
  \mu_N^{(1)}(\varphi):=\frac1{R_1(N)}\!\!\sum_{\substack{p<N\\ p,\,N-p\in\PP}}\!\!\varphi\Big(\tfrac{N-p}{N}\Big),
  \qquad
  \mu_N^{(2)}(\varphi):=\frac1{R_2(N)}\!\!\sum_{\substack{p<N\\ p\in\PP,\,N-p\in\SSemi}}\!\!\varphi\Big(\tfrac{N-p}{N}\Big),
\]
the averages of $\varphi$ against the type-$t$ location measures.

\begin{hypothesis}[Weighted prime and semiprime channel asymptotics]\label{hyp:weighted}
For every bounded $C^1$ test function $\varphi$ and almost all even $N$,
\[
  \sum_{\substack{p<N\\ p,\,N-p\in\PP}}\varphi\Big(\tfrac{N-p}{N}\Big)
  =(1+o(1))\,2C_2\,\sing(N)\,\frac{N}{\log^2N}\int_0^1\varphi(x)\,dx ,
\]
and, for each unblocked semiprime channel $q=rs$, uniformly in the range of $r$ used in the
paper,
\[
  \sum_{\substack{s\in\PP\\ N-rs\in\PP}}\varphi\Big(\tfrac{rs}{N}\Big)
  =(1+o(1))\,2C_2\,\sing(N)\,\frac{r-1}{r-2}\,\frac{N}{r\log^2N}\int_0^1\varphi(x)\,dx ,
\]
the channel omitted when $r\mid N$.
\end{hypothesis}

\begin{proposition}[Weak uniformity of the location measures; conditional]\label{prop:weakcont}
Under Hypothesis~\ref{hyp:weighted}, for almost all even $N$,
\[
  \mu_N^{(1)}(\varphi)=\int_0^1\varphi(x)\,dx+o(1),
  \qquad
  \mu_N^{(2)}(\varphi)=\int_0^1\varphi(x)\,dx+o(1) .
\]
Equivalently, the empirical measures of $q/N$ in the prime and semiprime channels converge
weakly to the uniform law on $(0,1)$, in the same smoothed/almost-all sense as the weighted
asymptotics.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
For the prime channel, the first weighted asymptotic with $\varphi\equiv1$ gives
$R_1(N)=(1+o(1))\,2C_2\sing(N)N/\log^2N$; dividing the two estimates yields
$\mu_N^{(1)}(\varphi)=\int_0^1\varphi+o(1)$. For the semiprime channel, writing $q=rs$ and
summing the per-channel asymptotic over the unblocked $r\le\sqrt N$,
\[
  \sum_{\substack{p<N\\ p\in\PP,\,N-p\in\SSemi}}\varphi\Big(\tfrac{N-p}{N}\Big)
  =(1+o(1))\,2C_2\sing(N)\,\frac{N}{\log^2N}
  \Big(\!\!\sum_{\substack{3\le r\le\sqrt N\\ r\nmid N}}\!\!\frac{r-1}{r-2}\frac1r\Big)\int_0^1\varphi(x)\,dx ,
\]
the parenthesis being the Mertens channel sum $W_f(N)$. Taking $\varphi\equiv1$ gives
the matching asymptotic for $R_2(N)$; dividing cancels both the inherited singular series
$\sing(N)$ and the channel sum, leaving $\mu_N^{(2)}(\varphi)=\int_0^1\varphi+o(1)$. This is
weak convergence against every bounded $C^1$ test function.
\end{proof}

This explains why the discontinuous pointwise representation sets nonetheless produce smooth
limiting clouds: the singular series governs the local \emph{density} of representations, but
after normalizing each measure it cancels---together with the Mertens channel sum in the
semiprime case---leaving the uniform spatial law. It is the conditional counterpart of the
weak-continuity diagnostics (supplement), and like the other appendix results it is
conditional on the stated weighted asymptotics, not an unconditional theorem about primes.


\subsection{A last-digit model for the carry excess}
\label{sec:carry-model}

The carry geometry of the carry observation in the main text can be partly explained by a local digit
calculation: prime-like summands are not uniform among terminal digits but restricted to
reduced residue classes, and this restriction alone creates a positive carry bias in the least
significant digit. Fix a base $b\ge2$ and let $U_b:=\{1\le u\le b-1:(u,b)=1\}$ be the reduced
residue digits modulo $b$. For digits $x,y\in\{0,\dots,b-1\}$ let
$\kappa_b(x,y):=\ones_{\{x+y\ge b\}}$ be the carry out of the least significant digit.

\begin{proposition}[Local carry excess; model]\label{prop:carry}
Let $X,Y$ be independent uniform digits in $\{0,\dots,b-1\}$ and $U,V$ independent uniform
digits in $U_b$. Then
\[
  \mathbb E\,\kappa_b(U,V)=\tfrac12+\tfrac12\Pr(U+V=b),\qquad
  \mathbb E\,\kappa_b(X,Y)=\frac{b-1}{2b},
\]
so $\mathbb E\,\kappa_b(U,V)-\mathbb E\,\kappa_b(X,Y)=\frac1{2b}+\tfrac12\Pr(U+V=b)>0$:
reduced-residue summands have a strictly larger least-digit carry probability than
unrestricted ones.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
For unrestricted digits,
$\#\{(x,y):0\le x,y\le b-1,\ x+y\ge b\}=\sum_{x=1}^{b-1}x=\tfrac{b(b-1)}2$, so
$\mathbb E\,\kappa_b(X,Y)=(b-1)/(2b)$. For reduced residues, the involution
$(u,v)\mapsto(b-u,b-v)$ maps $U_b^2$ to itself (since $(u,b)=1\iff(b-u,b)=1$), sends
$\{u+v<b\}$ bijectively to $\{u+v>b\}$, and fixes $\{u+v=b\}$; hence those two strict sets are
equinumerous and $\Pr(U+V\ge b)=\tfrac12+\tfrac12\Pr(U+V=b)$.
\end{proof}

The calculation is deliberately local: it does not prove the full carry statistics of actual
Goldbach pairs, nor assume independence of higher digits. It shows that the most basic prime
residue restriction already pushes the carry count up. In base $2$ both odd prime summands end
in $1$, so the least digit always carries; in larger bases the effect survives after averaging
over reduced classes. Since the total carry count obeys the Kummer--Legendre identity
$\#\mathrm{carries}_b(a,q)=(S_b(a)+S_b(q)-S_b(N))/(b-1)$ (the carry observation in the main text), the
proposition is a positive \emph{local} contribution to it, and the empirical excess of
the carry observation in the main text reads as its multi-digit continuation.

\begin{remark}[Fixed-$N$ caveat]
For a fixed terminal digit $N\bmod b$ the carry bias can depend on the residue class and need
not be positive in every class; the proposition is an averaged local-prime model, explaining
why a positive excess is natural \emph{on average} while the full statistic remains a global
multi-digit measurement. Like the other appendix results it is model-based, not a theorem
about the empirical carry law.
\end{remark}
