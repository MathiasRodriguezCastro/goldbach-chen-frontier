# Cover letter

**To:** The Editors, *Experimental Mathematics*
**Re:** Submission of *The Goldbach–Chen Frontier, Experimentally: A Singular-Series Cancellation and the Finite-Range Half-Power Artifact*
**Author:** Mathias Rodríguez Castro (ORCID 0009-0002-7235-7677)

Dear Editors,

I am pleased to submit the manuscript above for consideration in *Experimental Mathematics*.

**The experimental finding.** Separating the Goldbach count $R_1(N)$ (prime $q$) from the
Chen-surplus count $R_2(N)$ (semiprime $q$), a finite-range log–log regression assigns the
semiprime channel an apparent singular exponent $\beta_2\approx\tfrac12$ — strikingly different
from the $\beta_1=1$ of the prime channel. Computing to $X=10^9$ by a block-convolution sieve
shows this is **not** a structural half-power: the effective exponent drifts upward like
$\log\log X$.

**The explanation, and the precise conjecture.** An elementary but, to my knowledge,
unrecorded Euler-product identity shows that each unblocked semiprime channel inherits the
*same* singular series $\mathfrak S(N)$ that governs $R_1$, so $\mathfrak S$ cancels exactly in
the ratio $R_2/R_1$; the half-power is therefore an effective exponent inferred from the
covariance between the blocked channels $r\mid N$ and $\mathfrak S(N)$, with deficit
$1-\beta_2\asymp 1/\log\log N$. The almost-all linear asymptotic $R_2=\mathrm{main}(1+o(1))$ is
proved **unconditionally** by the circle method (the one arithmetic input, equidistribution of
semiprimes in progressions, is supplied; the result is not claimed as new mathematics); only the
passage to $\beta_2\to1$ is isolated as a precisely stated lower-tail conjecture, which I do
**not** prove. A "Status of the results" table and an "Open problems" section state exactly
what is proved, what is conditional, and what is conjectured.

**This is not a proof of Goldbach** (or of Chen), and does not claim to be — the contribution
is reproducible experimental evidence, a clarifying cancellation identity made explicit, and an
unconditional almost-all asymptotic, in the spirit of the journal.

**Reproducibility.** All figures, tables and quoted numbers are regenerated by an open-source
repository (`run_all.py`; pinned `requirements.txt`; per-module self-tests; the optional
selection-MIP driver is behind a flag so the artifact runs without a Gurobi license). A
versioned snapshot is archived on Zenodo, doi:10.5281/zenodo.20725701, and the GitHub
repository is public. The code and derived data are available to the referees.

The manuscript is single-author and has not been submitted elsewhere. I would be glad to
provide any further material.
